# Strength Coach — Evidence Base

## Core Principles

### Volume Dose-Response (Tier 1)
**Schoenfeld et al. (2017), Krieger (2010)**
- Hypertrophy is dose-dependent on weekly set volume per muscle group
- ~10 sets/week: good gains. 10–20 sets/week: near-maximal. >20: diminishing returns
- Minimum effective dose: ~5 sets/week/muscle group for maintenance (Krieger 2010)
- **Personal data**: back at 40% of target, legs at 31% → both potentially below maintenance threshold
- Priority: ensure back and legs reach minimum 8–10 sets/week before adding elsewhere

### RPE-Based Autoregulation (Tier 2)
**Helms et al. (2016), Zourdos et al. (2016)**
- RPE (Rating of Perceived Exertion) scale: 1–10, where 10 = failure
- Autoregulation produces comparable or better strength gains vs. fixed percentages
- Better adherence and lower injury risk in trained individuals
- Implementation: prescribe RPE targets (e.g., "3×8 @ RPE 8"), let load follow
- **Personal application**: on yellow recovery days, autoregulate down to RPE 7; on red, skip or deload

### Training Frequency (Tier 1–2)
**Schoenfeld et al. (2016)**
- 2× per week per muscle group produces greater hypertrophy than 1×
- Upper/lower split naturally achieves this with 4 sessions/week
- At current ~50% compliance (2 sessions/week actual), each muscle group may only get 1× per week → frequency is a problem

### Concurrent Training / Interference Effect (Tier 1–2)
**Wilson et al. (2012), Hickson (1980)**
- Running produces more interference than cycling for lower body hypertrophy
- Interference is volume-dependent: more running = more interference
- Mitigation strategies:
  - Strength before cardio (or 6h+ separation)
  - Prioritize rower over running for additional cardio
  - Keep running ≤6 sessions/month (personal threshold)
  - Adequate protein (1.6–2.2 g/kg — Jakob's 150g target is appropriate)

### Progressive Overload (Tier 1)
**Kraemer & Ratamess (2004)**
- Systematic increase in training stimulus over time is fundamental to adaptation
- Track across mesocycles (4–6 week blocks), not just session to session
- Methods: increase load, reps, sets, or decrease rest periods
- Deload every 4th week (reduce volume by 40–50%, maintain intensity)

### Bickel Maintenance Thresholds (Tier 2–3)
**Bickel et al. (2011)**
- Strength and muscle mass can be maintained with as little as 1/3 of the volume used to build them
- Frequency can drop to 1×/week if intensity is maintained
- **Application**: during exam-heavy periods, a maintenance protocol of 2 sessions/week at full intensity but reduced volume preserves gains
