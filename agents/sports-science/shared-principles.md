# Sports Science — Shared Principles

Both the running coach and strength coach reference these cross-cutting principles.

## Evidence Tier System
All training recommendations are tagged:
- **Tier 1**: Systematic reviews / meta-analyses
- **Tier 2**: Multiple RCTs with consistent findings
- **Tier 3**: Single RCTs or strong mechanistic evidence
- **Tier 4**: Observational / expert consensus

When personal data contradicts population-level evidence, flag it explicitly. No bro-science: if evidence is weak or mixed, say so.

## Recovery as the Governing Constraint
- Training stimulus + recovery = adaptation. Without recovery, training is just stress.
- HRV trends (not single readings) are the most reliable recovery indicator (Tier 2)
- Sleep is the single most impactful recovery variable (Tier 1)
- Recovery zones (green/yellow/red) gate what training is permissible

## Protein and Nutrition
- Target: 150g/day protein (vegetarian)
- Evidence supports 1.6–2.2 g/kg/day for hypertrophy (Tier 1: Morton et al. 2018)
- Timing: distribute across 4+ meals with ≥30g per meal (Tier 2–3)
- Leucine threshold: ~2.5g per meal for maximal MPS (Tier 2)
- Vegetarian consideration: combine protein sources for complete amino acid profile

## Concurrent Training Management
- Running interferes with lower body hypertrophy more than cycling (Wilson 2012, Tier 1)
- Personal threshold: LBM loss above ~6 runs/month
- Strength before cardio when same-day (or 6h+ separation)
- Rower as VO2max alternative reduces running-specific interference

## Deload and Periodization
- Deload every 3rd–4th week: reduce volume 40–50%, maintain intensity
- Block periodization: accumulation (volume) → intensification (load) → realization (peak)
- During exam periods: switch to maintenance protocol (Bickel thresholds)

## Key Distinction: Well-Established vs. Emerging
Well-established (act on these):
- Progressive overload, polarized endurance training, adequate protein, sleep prioritization

Emerging/contested (acknowledge uncertainty):
- Optimal HRV recovery thresholds (individual variation is large)
- Exact interference effect magnitude (depends on training status, volume, modality)
- vVO2max training superiority over 4×4 (limited head-to-head comparisons)
