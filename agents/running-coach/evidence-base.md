# Running Coach — Evidence Base

## Core Principles

### Polarized Training Distribution (Tier 1–2)
**Seiler (2010), Stöggl & Sperlich (2014)**
- ~80% of training volume at low intensity (below first ventilatory threshold)
- ~20% at high intensity (above second ventilatory threshold)
- Threshold training (moderate intensity) minimized — "black hole" avoidance
- Meta-analysis shows polarized outperforms threshold-based for VO2max improvement
- **Personal data conflict**: 55/58 historical runs were NOT zone 2 → near-complete inversion of polarized model

### Norwegian 4×4 Protocol (Tier 2)
**Helgerud et al. (2007)**
- 4 × 4 min at 90–95% HRmax, 3 min active recovery at 70% HRmax
- Produced ~7.2% VO2max improvement over 8 weeks in moderately trained subjects
- Effective for individuals in the 40–55 ml/kg/min range (Jakob's current zone)
- Frequency: 2–3× per week in study; for hybrid athlete, 1× per week is sufficient

### vVO2max Intervals (Tier 2–3)
**Billat et al. (2001)**
- Velocity at VO2max (vVO2max): the slowest speed that elicits VO2max
- 30/30 protocol: 30s at vVO2max, 30s at 50% vVO2max
- Accumulates more time at VO2max than continuous running
- Best used in Build phase, 1× per 2 weeks alongside 4×4 sessions

### Cadence and Ground Contact Time (Tier 2)
**Heiderscheit et al. (2011)**
- Increasing step rate by 5–10% reduces impact loading, hip adduction, and braking forces
- Current: 150 spm → target 170 spm (13% increase — implement gradually over 6–8 weeks)
- GCT 262ms → target <240ms as cadence improves
- Cue: "quick, light feet" during easy runs; metronome app for tempo runs

### Concurrent Training Interference (Tier 1–2)
**Wilson et al. (2012), Hickson (1980)**
- Running volume negatively affects hypertrophy more than cycling
- Personal threshold: LBM loss detected above ~6 runs/month in Jakob's data
- Mitigation: prioritize rower for additional VO2max stimulus, keep running volume low
- When combining: strength before cardio, or separate by 6+ hours

### Rower as VO2max Alternative (Tier 3–4)
- Concept 2 rower produces comparable cardiac output to running
- 4×4 protocol transferable to rower (match HR targets, not pace)
- 8×30s sprints with 90s recovery: effective HIIT alternative
- Advantage: zero impact loading, preserves running volume cap
- Limitation: less sport-specific for running performance (no running economy gains)
